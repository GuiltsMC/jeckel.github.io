Sukhoi's Su-57; The Life Sized Paper Plane

Meet the Sukhoi Su-57, also known as the "Felon" - sounds menacing, right? It's a nifty twin-engine, single seat stealth aircraft that Sukhoi developed. With its thrust-vectoring nozzles, this bad boy can do some super-maneuvers that'll make you dizzy. Its engines generate between 24,054 and 35,556 pounds of thrust EACH - that's some serious oomph! This baby can even supercruise, meaning it can go faster than the speed of sound when cruising. While it's not as stealthy as the F-22, it still has some cool stealth features. It's also got fancy avionics and can carry a whole lot of stuff - up to ~22,000 lbs, to be precise. And let's not forget its max take off weight of ~77,000 lbs, and fuel tank capacity of ~2,700 gallons. That's a whole lot of plane for a whole lot of fun!


Why the Su-57 is inferior to the F-22

So, the Su-57's got some slick new aerodynamics that put the old Flanker to shame. And apparently it gives the F-22 a run for its money - or so they say. I mean, I have my doubts, but who am I to judge? The Su-57's got some fancy canted vertical stabilizers and trapezoid wings with all the flaps and ailerons you could want. Plus, it's got thrust vectoring and leading-edge root extensions that make it crazy maneuverable - if a little unstable. But hey, who needs stability when you're dogfighting, right? Speaking of which, the F-22 was literally built to be the king of the skies. It's got all the bells and whistles - stealth, anti-ground capabilities, signals intelligence...you name it. And it's a tricky bugger to spot on radar, too. So, if you're looking for a good old-fashioned beyond visual range fight, the F-22's your man. But let's say you want to get up close and personal. In that case, it'd be a real toss-up between the Su-57 and the F-22. I mean, those Archers on the Su-57 might give the F-22's AIM-9s a run for their money, but at the end of the day, it's all about the pilot. After all, the plane's only half the battle - unless you're fighting a drone, in which case you're probably out of luck. But let's not go there.
